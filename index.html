The Beginning

That question was simple, and human, and wouldn't leave me alone. Following it honestly — without the permission of institutions or the guardrails of formal education — led me somewhere I didn't expect. It led me to energy. To consciousness. To the shape of the universe itself.

"We are not just living in the universe.
We are training to become it."

What I've built is a theory of how universes cycle, evolve, and pass information forward — through black holes and white holes, through dark matter, through sunlight written into our DNA. It's also a manifesto about what we are inside that cycle — not passengers, but contributors. Not accidents, but participants in something that has been turning long before us and will continue long after.

I'm not asking you to believe it. I'm asking you to follow the reasoning and see where it takes you. Because the question of what happens when we die doesn't belong to science alone, or to religion alone. It belongs to anyone willing to think seriously about it.

I didn't wait for permission to think seriously about it.
This is the result.

✦

Where It All Meets

Two bodies of work. One started with the cosmos — the spiral curvature of gravity, black holes as compression engines, white holes as the seeds of new universes. The other started with the human — what consciousness is, what death means, what we are here to do.

They were always the same question asked from opposite ends.

"The philosophy needed the physics to give it structure.
The physics needed the philosophy to give it meaning.
Together they became one final theory."

The Azzopardi Framework is where both meet — a single unified model that runs from the birth of a universe to the death of a human being and back again. Not two ideas sitting side by side, but one complete and continuous spiral.
